Middle stage Alzheimers is commonly the longest stage of Alzheimers and can last many years. As the disease worsens the more care the person will require. During this stage the symtoms become fairly clear. In addition the symtoms that a person experiences at this stage become more life altering and intense. The damage to nerve cells in the brain will progress making it difficult for the person to express there thoughts and carry out basic tasks without help. Symptoms can vary based on a person and there condition.

The most common symtoms of Middle stage Alzheimers include:

Forgetfulness of events and personal history/memories
Mood swings withdrawn from social, mental and difficult situations
Unable to recall personal information (contact info, address, schools attended)
Confusion about time or place
Change in sleep patterns from day to night
Person may experience bladder/ bowel issues
Personality, behavioral changes
Person may experience delusions compulsive and redundant behavior

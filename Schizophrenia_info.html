Known Causes of Schizophrenia

Schizophrenia is one of many multi-factorial disorder. This is because researchers belive that Schizophrenia is caused by combination of genetics, brain chemistry and environmental factors. Schizophrenia is a phsyciatric Illness. Schizophrenia can be caused from problems with certain naturally occurring brain chemicals including neurotransmitters like dopamine and glutamine. Neuro transmitters carry signals from one neuron to another target cell that recieves the signal.

In addition having a family member can substancially increase ones risk of devloping Schizophrenia. Furthermore the causes can even be as complex as birth/ pregnancy complications that occured. Symptoms

In men, schizophrenia symptoms usally start in the early to mid-20s. In women, symptoms typically begin in the late 20s. Schizophrenia is not commonally diagnosed in kids or people older than 45. Symptoms all vary on a person and are bound to worsen over time. The symptoms found in Schizophrenia mostly involve issues with thinking, behavior and emotions. Signs and symptoms also usually involve delusions, hallucinations or disorganized speech, and reflect an impaired ability to function. Schizophrenia has 3 different stages called prodromal, active, and residual.

What parts of the brain are affected by Schizophrenia

The structures/ areas of the brain that Schizophrenia affects is the frontal, temporal, and thalamus regions. The fronal lobe is ythe largest of the brain 4 regions in the Cerebral cortex which is the outer layer of the brain. The outer layer of the brain in responsible for a number of functions such as, memory, judgment, motor tasks(voluntary movement), and social appropriateness. The temporal lobe is also one of the major 4 lobes. The temporal lobe is responsible for functions such as auditory stimuli (hearing), memory, and emotions. In addition it is found that Schizophrenia patients have a lack of white matter which is responsible for connecting the different brain regions internally.The areas that are affected due to Schizophrenia explain the phsycosis symptom experienced by Schizophrenia patients.

Prodomal stage (stage one)

The prodomal stage of Schizophrenia occurs before phsycotic symptoms appear.Duringthis stage a person begins to experience behavioral and thinking changes eventually progress to phsychosis. The early symptoms that occur in the prodomal stage of Schizophrenia usually involve non specific symtoms that also occur in other mental illness, like depression. For this reason indentifying people in the Preodomal stage of Schizophrenia is difficult.

Symptoms in the prodomal stage of Schizophrenia most commonly include:

social isolation
lack of motivation
Anxiety
Quick temper
difficulty concentrating
changes to ones normal routine
sleep problems
Lack of hygiene erratic behavior
Mild or poorly formed hallucinations

Active Stage (stage two)

Active stage is the second phase of Schizophrenia. In this stage Schizophrenia patients begin to experience symptoms of phsychosis including hallucinations, delusions and paranoia. Active schizophrenia/ phsycosis includes more obvious symptoms. In addition, in this phase the symptoms begin to worsen and a person requires more care.

The symptom involved in Active Schizophrenia/ Phsycocis are:

Hallucinations-seeing, hearing, smelling, or feeling things that others do not
Delusions-false notions or ideas that a person believes even when proven wrong
Confused and disorganized thoughts
Disordered or jumbled speech
Excessive or useless movement
Wandering
mumbling
Laughing to oneself
Numbing of emotions

Residual Schizophrenia (stage three)

Ironically in the Risidual stage of Schizophrenia a peroson experiences less symptoms or even less severe symptoms than in the active stage. Normally people in this phase do not experience symptoms such as hallucinations and delusions. The Risidual is fairly similar to the prodomal stage of Schizophrenia.

The symptoms of the Risidual stage of Schizophrenia include:

social withdrawal
Difficulty concentrating
Difficulty planning and participating in activities
Reduced or absent facial expressions
Flat, monotone voice (remains at the smae tone, pitch, volume and speed. Doesnt reflect any changes in mood or attitude.)
General lack of interest in anything

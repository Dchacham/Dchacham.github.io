Late stage Alzheimers(stage three)

Late stage Alzheimers is the most severe stage. People suffering from Alzheimers will lose there ability to repond to there evironment. Carrying out a conversation will be exeedingly difficult for a person. In addition, a person may begin to move unctrollably. As a person memory continues to decline they will experience significant personality changes. Late stage Alzheimers requires intense and constant care.

Although each person may experience symptoms differently the most common symtoms/ difficulties found at this stage include:

Losing awareness of recent situations/ experiences (surroundings)
Difficulty communicating
Becoming more at risk for infections especially pneumonia
Loses many physical abilities, including walking, sitting, eating.
May lose bowel and bladder control
May be able to say some words or phrases, but not have a conversation
Needs help with all activities all of the time
Is unaware of recent experiences and of his or her surroundings
